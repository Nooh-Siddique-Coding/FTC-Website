Odometry Perception

We use odometry in FTC because it lets the robot know its precise location on the field throughout the match, especially during autonomous. Without it, the robot would rely only on motor power and timing, which can easily lead to drifting, misalignment, or missed objectives. Odometry provides accurate X/Y position and heading, allowing the robot to correct its movement, follow paths smoothly, and consistently score game elements in the right spots. In short, we use odometry so the robot can move with precision, reliability, and repeatability, even when the field or wheels aren‚Äôt perfect.

Adaptive Intake

Our GripForce gecko intake uses a tacky, high-friction surface inspired by gecko adhesion to pull game pieces in quickly and securely. The sticky material creates strong grip without needing a lot of downward pressure, which helps the intake stay gentle on pixels while still being fast and reliable. As soon as the intake touches a game piece, the gecko pads latch on and guide it smoothly into the robot, reducing jams and misalignment. This system lets us collect from almost any angle and maintain control even during fast driving, making our cycling much quicker and more consistent.

Extended Container

Our funnel container with gecko wheels is designed to smoothly center and secure pixels as they enter the robot. The funnel shape guides the game pieces toward the middle, while the gecko wheels provide gentle but powerful grip to pull them in and keep them stable. Because the wheels use a tacky, adhesive-like surface, they can control the pixels without slipping or needing high speed, which reduces jams and keeps the intake consistent. Excellent Outtake

Our robot uses a hooded flywheel as its main launching system, designed to give every shot consistent power and accuracy. The hood helps control the angle of the projectile, guiding it along a smooth, curved path so each launch comes out the same way. The flywheel spins at a high, regulated RPM, and the hood ensures that the ring or pixel makes full contact for maximum energy transfer. This setup lets us fine-tune our shooting distance, tighten our grouping, and maintain reliable performance even during fast cycles. Overall, the hood flywheel gives us a balanced combination of speed, precision, and repeatability in scoring.

Mecanum Wheels

Our robot uses mecanum grip wheels to get precise and smooth omnidirectional movement while still maintaining strong traction. The specialized rollers let the robot strafe, rotate, and move diagonally with ease, giving us full control in tight spaces. Because these are grip-style mecanums, they provide better friction on the field than standard smooth rollers, which improves acceleration, stability, and pushing power. This extra grip also helps our odometry stay accurate by reducing wheel slip. Overall, the mecanum grip wheels give us the agility we need for quick cycling while keeping our movements controlled and consistent.
